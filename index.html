Understanding the Diagram

Points: Numbered circles on the diagram. Each point has a unique number and color.

Cells: Colored regions represent Voronoi cells. Each cell in a first-order diagram contains all points that are closer to a specific point than to any other point.

Connections: Dashed lines show the relationship between points and their cells.

Higher-order diagrams: When the order is greater than 1, cells show numbers indicating the set of points that are the n closest points for that region.
